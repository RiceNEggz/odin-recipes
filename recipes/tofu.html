Korean Soft Tofu Stew

Description

This Korean Soft Tofu Stew is a rather quick recipe for a nice warm meal. It hits all of the dietary needs for protein, fats, and carbohydrates, to provide lasting energy throughout the day. To top it off, this stew is absolutely delicious!

The preperation for this dish only requires the chopping of vegetables, but can be completed in one pot!

Ingredients

  • 1 Package Silken Tofu
  • 1/3 Cup thinly sliced kimchi
  • 3 ounces pork or beef
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 1 to 3 teaspoons red chili pepper flakes
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 cup water or anchovy broth
  • 2 tablespoons juice from kimchi
  • pinch of black pepper
  • 1 scallion, finely chopped
  • 1 egg

Cooking Instructions

  1. Cut the meat and kimchi into small strips, or whatever size is comfortable for whoever is eating it. I personally like larger chunks of meat in my stews.
  2. Add the sliced kimchi, meat, chili pepper flakes, garlic and sesame oil into a pot and place it over medium heat.
  3. Stir-fry until the meat is almost cooked, or cook to your desired crispiness, for more texture.
  4. Pour in water or broth, and the kimchi juice.
  5. Let the broth and stew boil for 3 to 4 minutes.
  6. Add the soft tofu in the pot, and season with the salted shrimp or salt to taste.
  7. Add the pinch of black pepper and let cook for 4 to 5 minutes.
  8. Garnish the dish with the chopped scallion, and take off the heat.
  9. Crack the egg into the boiling soup before serving.
