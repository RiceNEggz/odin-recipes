Quick and Easy Pork Larb

Description

Pork Larb can be made into a quick and simple recipe, if you're on a time crunch. It's essentially a short fry of pork, then dressed in a seasoned sauce. Larb can be placed over rice, in a lettuce cup, or any other choice of carbohydrate. (Though I would suggest sticking to rice, as it absorbs any extra seasoning really well.)

Unfortunately, Larb utilizes fish sauce, which for many, might be a deal breaker. Fish sauce, though pungent, provides a nice vinegar and umami blend, that really enhances the flavor of the meat. Larb, focuses on the meat, but adds texture and color with the additional garnishes.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of pork
  • 2 tablespoons of fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of lime juice
  • 1/4 finely chopped red onion
  • 1/4 cup of thai basil (whole leaf)

Cooking Instructions

  1. Place the pork on a pan over high heat, and cook until dark brown. (Cooking times can vary based on desired crispness of the pork.
  2. While the pork is cooking, mix the fish sauce, sugar and lime juice in a bowl.
  3. Adjust the sauce to your preference, based on acidity, sweetness, and saltiness.
  4. Add the chopped red onion and basil into the sauce, and mix thoroughly.
  5. Once the pork is done cooking, mix the pork into the sauce and serve.
